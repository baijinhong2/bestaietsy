## 1. What Etsy's AI Content & Authenticity Policy actually says in 2026

The policy is in plain language and has been stable since 2024, with 2026 enforcement getting tighter. The [official text on the Etsy Seller Handbook](https://www.etsy.com/seller-handbook/article/AI-generated-content-policy) does three things: (1) it allows AI-generated and AI-assisted content on Etsy — AI is not banned, full stop; (2) it requires that every listing accurately represent the work, so you cannot present AI-generated content as fully handmade if your shop branding implies otherwise; (3) it requires that all AI use comply with the rest of Etsy's policies, including the [6/2 children policy](https://www.etsy.com/legal/policy/handmade-policy), the 8/11 fur ban, the [prohibited items list](https://www.etsy.com/legal/policy/prohibited-items), and the IP/trademark rules. A single sentence at the end of the description is the standard: "Description drafted with AI assistance and reviewed by the shop owner" or "Listing copy generated with ChatGPT and edited for accuracy". The exact wording is not mandated, but the disclosure has to be visible to the buyer.

**Thing 4 — Categorize AI-generated work as 'designed by', not 'handmade'.** Etsy's [handmade policy](https://www.etsy.com/legal/policy/handmade-policy) treats AI as a tool, not a person. AI-generated work is designed-by, not made-by. Shop sections, tags, and descriptions need to reflect this — the seller can still claim the design (the prompt, the curation, the selection) but the final image is AI-generated and the listing has to say so.

AI images of a physical product that does not match the listing are misrepresentation, and Etsy's buyer-protection system can trigger refunds in that case. Etsy has not publicly disclosed a formal AI-detection mechanism or a specific AI-score threshold. In practice, three signals trigger reviews: (1) **buyer reports** when a listing reads obviously AI-generated, (2) **policy flags** when the AI draft includes keywords that violate the 6/2 children policy, 8/11 fur ban, or prohibited items list, and (3) **quality audits** on shops that suddenly publish a wave of listings with similar copy patterns. AI-generated means AI drafted the full title, description, or image, and a human reviewed it — one-line disclosure is the current best practice.
