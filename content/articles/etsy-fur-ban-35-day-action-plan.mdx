## What changed (and why it matters more than you think)

Etsy published the 2026 Animal Fur Policy Update in April 2026, with an effective date of August 11, 2026. The new rules bring three meaningful shifts from previous Etsy policy.

First, the policy covers all real animal fur - raw materials (mink, fox, rabbit, raccoon, chinchilla, lynx, coyote, sable), garments (coats, jackets, outerwear), and accessories (hats, scarves, shawls, bags with fur trim). This is broader than Etsy's existing endangered species ban, which only covers CITES-listed animals.

Second - and this is the key disruption for vintage sellers - **the vintage exemption is removed**. Etsy previously allowed 20+ year old animal fur under its vintage category. Effective August 11, even 100-year-old mink coats, antique fur stoles, and pre-1970s fur hats are banned. This change was driven by the Coalition to Abolish Fur Trade (CAFT), which has publicly committed to pushing other platforms toward similar restrictions.

Third, the policy includes a no-warning automated compliance scan on August 12. Listings containing banned keywords or images will be removed without individual notice. Etsy will email sellers a list of removed listings, but the first violation is already on record.

## Who is affected

Etsy's 2026 fur ban hits three seller cohorts hardest.

**Severely affected** - vintage clothing sellers with coats and jackets, vintage accessory sellers handling hats and scarves, sellers with decorative fur trim on outerwear, and mixed-vintage shops with significant inventory. Conservative estimates put several thousand Etsy shops in this group.

**Moderately affected** - shops with occasional fur items (less than 10% of inventory). These sellers can clean up quickly with bulk AI tools.

**Not affected** - digital download sellers, POD sellers, pure craft sellers, and sellers who work only in leather, wool, or shearling.

## What's still allowed

The complete exemption list is short but covers most natural fiber apparel.

**Allowed materials:** Leather (cow, pig, sheep, lamb, suede), wool of all sheep varieties, sheepskin and shearling, mohair, angora, cashmere. Animal specimens (taxidermy) require separate Etsy pre-approval but are generally allowed when disclosed properly. Faux fur (synthetic, plant-based, recycled fibers) is fully permitted - even recommended for sellers targeting ethical and sustainability-focused buyers. Fiber art without animal hair is also allowed.

**Gray areas requiring judgment:** Mixed-material products where animal fur is the dominant material are banned. The practical test: if removing the fur would destroy the product's purpose, it is fur-dominant and banned. If removing it leaves a useful product, the main item is likely compliant. Upcycled fur products and handmade fur items are still banned because the material itself is the issue.

## The 35-day action plan

This is the core of the guide: a day-by-day task list for the next five weeks. Each week has a clear outcome. Skip a week and the next week becomes harder.

### Week 1 (July 7-14): Audit and categorize

**Goal:** Know exactly what's in your shop that needs to change.

**Day 1-2:** Export all listings as CSV from Etsy Shop Manager (Settings → Download Data). Include SKU, title, description, tags, materials, and images. Save to a working folder.

**Day 3-4:** Run [Roketfy AI Listing Checker](/tools/roketfy) over your exported CSV. The checker scans titles, descriptions, and tags for policy red-line words: mink, fox, rabbit, raccoon, chinchilla, lynx, coyote, sable, fur coat, fur stole, fur trim, real fur, authentic fur, vintage mink. Expect a flagged-listings report within 2-3 minutes for a 200-listing shop.

**Day 5-7:** Categorize each flagged listing into four buckets: (1) delete entirely - clearly real animal fur, (2) rewrite - can be reclassified as faux fur or vintage acrylic, (3) borderline - mixed-material items needing closer inspection, (4) keep - false positives from the keyword scan. Estimate inventory value at risk for each bucket. This is your baseline.

**Week 1 deliverable:** A spreadsheet with every flagged listing categorized, total inventory at risk, and an estimated rewrite workload.

### Week 2 (July 14-21): Delete and rewrite

**Goal:** Zero banned listings on your shop by end of week.

**Day 8-10:** Delete all listings in bucket 1 (clearly banned). Important: use Delete, not Delist. Delist keeps the listing in your draft folder and can mislead both you and Etsy's scan. Deletion is permanent and removes the listing from Etsy's index.

**Day 11-12:** Use [Roketfy AI Writer](/tools/roketfy) to bulk-rewrite bucket 2 listings. The AI Writer takes your original listing and produces a compliant version: "vintage mink coat" becomes "vintage acrylic faux fur coat 1970s," "fox stole" becomes "faux fur stole - not real animal fur." For a 100-listing rewrite, this takes 2-3 hours versus 2-3 weeks manually.

**Day 13-14:** Inspect bucket 3 borderline items manually. For mixed-material products, decide between (a) removing the fur trim and selling the main item, (b) replacing real fur with faux fur trim, or (c) deleting. Update each listing accordingly.

**Week 2 deliverable:** All bucket 1 and 3 listings resolved. Bucket 2 listings rewritten and republished. Run a second [Roketfy scan](/tools/roketfy) to confirm zero remaining policy red-line words.

### Week 3 (July 21-28): Source alternatives

**Goal:** Have new inventory sourced for the categories you're pivoting into.

**Day 15-17:** Use [EtsyHunt Product Research](/tools/etsyhunt) to identify top-selling categories in faux fur, wool, shearling, and leather. Filter by Etsy category (vintage coats, vintage accessories, outerwear) and look at the top 100 best-sellers in each. Note the price ranges, color patterns, and listing structures that work.

**Day 18-19:** Use [eRank Keyword Explorer](/tools/erank) to find compliant keywords with real search volume. Examples: "faux fur coat vintage" (high volume, low competition), "wool blend vintage scarf" (steady traffic), "shearling jacket women" (sustained demand). Add these to your keyword bank for Week 4.

**Day 20-21:** Contact your suppliers or vintage sourcing channels. If you sell vintage outerwear, pivot sourcing toward 1970s-1980s acrylic faux fur coats, vintage wool overcoats, and shearling-lined leather jackets. These categories are growing in search demand and remain fully compliant.

**Week 3 deliverable:** 10-20 new inventory items sourced (or queued for sourcing), keyword bank populated with 30+ compliant keywords.

### Week 4 (July 28-August 4): Generate and list

**Goal:** New compliant listings live on your shop, with AI-generated product photos.

**Day 22-24:** Generate lifestyle product photos for your new faux fur and wool inventory using AI tools. Midjourney, DALL-E, or Stageflow can create on-model and lifestyle shots without using real animal fur photos that would trigger Etsy's image-based scan. Include 5-7 photos per new listing.

**Day 25-28:** Use [Roketfy AI Writer](/tools/roketfy) again to draft the new listings. Title format: "[Era] [Material] [Item Type]" (e.g., "1970s Faux Fur Coat Vintage Brown"). Description: 3 paragraphs covering era, condition, and styling. Tags: 13 tags covering era, material, color, occasion, and style. Publish each listing with the AI-generated photos.

**Week 4 deliverable:** 10-20 new compliant listings published. Run [Originality.ai](/tools/originality-ai) compliance check on each one to confirm zero policy red-line words before publishing.

### Week 5 (August 4-11): Final verification

**Goal:** Zero risk when Etsy's August 12 scan runs.

**Day 29-31:** Run one final [Roketfy scan](/tools/roketfy) across your entire shop. The scan should report zero policy violations. If it reports any, resolve them immediately - you have 7-10 days.

**Day 32-34:** Open Etsy Seller Support chat and ask for a manual pre-check on your shop. Etsy support can flag any items they would consider borderline before the August 12 automated scan runs. This is a free service and most sellers who ask get a useful response within 24 hours.

**Day 35 (August 10):** Final review. Walk through your entire shop manually. Confirm no listings contain real fur references. Confirm all images are clearly synthetic, plant-based, or vintage acrylic. Confirm Star Seller metrics are protected (no recent removals).

**Week 5 deliverable:** Zero-policy-violation shop ready for August 11.

- Yes → Step 2
