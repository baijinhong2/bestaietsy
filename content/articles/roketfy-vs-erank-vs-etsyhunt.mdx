## Roketfy — the AI listing writer

**What it does:** Roketfy is a Turkish-built Etsy platform that launched in 2022 and is now used by approximately 100,000 sellers. The core product is the AI listing writer — upload a product image and a one-line description, and Roketfy generates a complete listing: SEO title (under 140 characters), 13 tags, and a 150-200 word description. It supports 25+ languages including English, Spanish, French, German, and Chinese.

**Best for:**

- New sellers with 50+ listings who need to scale copy quickly
- Multilingual sellers who want one listing translated into 10+ languages
- Time-poor sellers who would rather approve AI drafts than write from scratch
- Shops doing 50-200 new listings per quarter

**Pros:**

- AI Writer quality is the highest in the Etsy tool space (4 out of 5 drafts were publishable in our test with light editing)
- $5.99/month annual is 70% cheaper than eRank Pro
- 25+ language support is unmatched among the three tools
- 7-day free trial with no credit card required

**Cons:**

- Database (~5M listings) is much smaller than eRank's
- AI Writer occasionally produces keyword-stuffed descriptions that need editing
- No permanent free plan — only the 7-day trial
- Community and support resources are thinner than eRank's

**Pricing:** Starter $5.99/month (annual) or $9.99/month (monthly). Pro $19.99/month. Hobby plan $39/month billed monthly.

The Chrome extension shows keyword data directly on Etsy.com while you browse.

**Best for:**

- All sellers (the free plan genuinely works for beginners)
- Data-driven sellers running 500+ listings
- Agencies managing multiple shops
- Vintage and handmade sellers needing category-level depth

**Cons:**

- Interface feels dated compared to Roketfy

## EtsyHunt — the product research tool

**What it does:** EtsyHunt launched in 2018 from a Chinese team and tracks 27 million Etsy products plus Amazon and Shopify data. The product-first research is its differentiator: filter by sales velocity, review count, price, country, and category to find niches or validate design ideas. The Chrome extension works on Etsy, Amazon, and Shopify product pages. EtsyHunt also has a fully translated Chinese version — useful if you sell across both markets.

**Best for:**

- POD sellers researching winning designs before creating
- Cross-platform sellers validating an Etsy idea on Amazon or Shopify
- Chinese sellers selling globally
- Sellers who want product-first data instead of keyword-first data

**Pros:**

- $3.99/month annual is the cheapest of the three paid tools
- 27M+ product database with deep filtering
- Only one of the three with cross-platform data (Etsy + Amazon + Shopify)
- Chinese-language version is fully translated

**Cons:**

- No AI Writer as of mid-2026
- Database updates monthly vs eRank's weekly
- Customer support is slower for English-only users (24h Chinese, 48h English)
- Smaller user community than eRank

**Pricing:** Basic $3.99/month (annual) or $5.99/month (monthly). Pro $9.99/month. Business $19.99/month.

## Which one to pick — 5 real scenarios

### Scenario 1: You are a new seller with fewer than 50 listings and under $1,000/month revenue

Pick **eRank Free**. The free plan is permanent, the keyword data is the most accurate of the three, and at this stage you do not yet need AI to write listings — 5 listings a week is something you can handle manually while you learn what your buyers respond to. Revisit paid tools when monthly revenue crosses $1,000.

### Scenario 2: You have 500+ listings and $10,000+/month revenue

Pick **eRank Pro** ($19.99/month) plus **Roketfy Starter** ($5.99/month annual) — total $25.98/month. The combination gives you bulk-edit and competitor tracking (eRank's strength) plus AI Writer for new listings (Roketfy's strength). This is the workflow most agencies use.

### Scenario 3: You are a POD (print on demand) seller

Pick **EtsyHunt Pro** ($9.99/month). POD sellers need product-first research, not keyword-first — you want to find a winning niche before designing, not optimize listings after the fact. EtsyHunt's product filters and cross-platform validation (Amazon, Shopify) are unique in this comparison. Skip Roketfy unless you also write long-form descriptions.

### Scenario 4: You specialize in vintage or handmade

Pick **eRank Basic** (free). Vintage and handmade categories have granular subcategories (1980s denim, mid-century ceramics, etc.) and eRank's category-level keyword data is the deepest of the three. Roketfy and EtsyHunt are weaker at this niche depth.

### Scenario 5: You run an agency or manage 5+ shops

Pick **eRank Pro** ($19.99/month) plus **EtsyHunt Pro** ($9.99/month) — total $29.98/month. You need cross-shop comparison and cross-platform data, which is exactly what these two provide together. Skip Roketfy for agency use — it is single-shop focused.

## Final verdict — what we actually recommend

If you can only pick one, **eRank Free** is the correct starting point for 90% of Etsy sellers in 2026. It is permanently free, the data is the most accurate, and 2 million sellers have validated it. Once monthly revenue crosses $5,000, add Roketfy's $5.99/month AI Writer to accelerate new listing creation.

If you are POD or cross-platform, replace eRank Free with **EtsyHunt Pro** at $9.99/month — POD sellers need product-first research more than keyword research.

If your budget is under $10/month, the three-tool combo (**eRank Free + Roketfy $5.99 + EtsyHunt $3.99 = $9.98/month annual**) covers every workflow and costs less than eRank Pro alone.

What we do **not** recommend: paying $19.99/month for eRank Pro as a single tool if you have under 500 listings. The bulk-edit features will not save you enough time to justify the cost at that scale.
