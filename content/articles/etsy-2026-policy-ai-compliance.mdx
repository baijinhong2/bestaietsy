## The four 2026 policies: what changed and when

The 2026 policy window is the densest in Etsy's history. Treat the four events as a single combined shift, not four independent rules — most sellers are affected by two or three of them simultaneously, and the AI compliance method has to cover all of them at once.

### Policy 1 — Etsy children and infants product policy (effective 6/2/2026)

The children and infants product policy took effect on June 2, 2026 and is the most prescriptive of the four 2026 rules. The policy requires age-range disclosure on every children-targeted listing, restricts imagery that depicts minors in product photos, and prohibits unsubstantiated safety or developmental claims in titles and descriptions. Listings that violate the policy are removed without warning, and repeat violations trigger shop-level review.

**Who is affected:** any seller with a listing that targets a children-related search term, regardless of whether the listing is in a children-specific category. A "wooden toy car" listing in the Toys & Games category is in scope. A "nursery wall art" listing in the Home Decor category is also in scope because the term "nursery" triggers children-related search intent. The [Etsy Children and Infants Product Policy](https://www.etsy.com/legal/policy/children-and-infant-products) is the official source.

**What is still allowed:** listings with no children-related keywords, listings in non-children categories, and listings that include the required age-range disclosure. AI-generated imagery is permitted as long as the imagery does not depict identifiable minors — illustrated or stylized children are allowed when labeled as AI-generated.

**AI compliance method:** every children-related listing must pass a two-step scan. Step 1 is a keyword check via [Roketfy's Listing Checker](https://roketfy.com/listing-checker), which flags age-range omissions, unverified safety claims, and policy-triggering phrases. Step 2 is an AI detection check via [Originality.ai](https://originality.ai/) to confirm that AI-generated imagery is properly disclosed. The full 5-step workflow is in our [Etsy Children Products Policy AI Compliance guide](/blog/etsy-children-products-policy-ai-compliance).

### Policy 2 — Etsy 2026 seller cleanup (effective 6/4/2026)

On June 4, 2026, Etsy completed the largest seller purge in the platform's history. The cleanup targeted Chinese sellers using Payoneer routing loopholes to operate storefronts that appeared to be US or EU-based, bypassing Etsy's geographic identity rules. The active seller count dropped from 7 million in Q4 2024 to 5.6 million by Q2 2026 — a 20% reduction in three quarters, per [Marketplace Pulse](https://www.marketplacepulse.com/stats/etsy-active-sellers).

**Who is affected:** primarily Chinese sellers operating under false geographic identities, and secondarily, US and EU sellers whose search rankings shifted because of the reduced competition. The 1.4M removed sellers had been inflating search volume on generic keywords, which means the surviving sellers now compete in a less crowded but more competitive long-tail environment.

**What is still allowed:** any seller operating a single verified geographic identity. There is no new paperwork for compliant sellers — the cleanup only removed sellers who were already in violation of Etsy's geographic identity rules.

**AI compliance method:** the cleanup is not directly an AI policy, but it is part of the same 2026 trust-and-safety enforcement wave. The AI angle is that surviving sellers should use SEO tools like [eRank](https://erank.com/keyword-explorer) to capture the long-tail traffic that the removed sellers were siphoning off. Generic keywords that were previously saturated are now realistic to rank for with AI-optimized listings.

### Policy 3 — UK regulatory operating fee (effective 6/22/2026, 0.32% to 0.48%)

The UK regulatory operating fee rose from 0.32% to 0.48% on June 22, 2026. The fee funds UK digital markets regulation enforcement and is added on top of Etsy's standard 6.5% transaction fee and $0.20 listing fee.

**Who is affected:** UK-based sellers on every UK sale, and secondarily, non-UK sellers whose orders ship to UK addresses. For a UK seller making £10,000/month in UK sales, the increase from 0.32% to 0.48% adds £16/month in fees. The math is small for low-volume sellers and material for shops doing £50k+/month.

**What is still allowed:** the fee is non-negotiable and applies to all UK sales. There is no opt-out, no category exemption, and no way to pass the fee to the buyer without violating Etsy's pricing rules.

**AI compliance method:** AI tools offset the fee by lifting conversion rates. A 5% conversion lift on a £10,000/month shop is worth £500/month in incremental revenue, which covers the £16/month fee 30x over. The specific tool mix for UK sellers is covered in the use case matrix below — the short version is that [eRank](https://erank.com/keyword-explorer) Pro for trend monitoring and [Roketfy](https://roketfy.com/listing-checker) for listing optimization are the highest-ROI picks for UK shops.

### Policy 4 — Etsy animal fur policy (effective 8/11/2026)

The animal fur policy is the most disruptive of the four 2026 events because it removes the pre-2026 vintage exemption. Every animal fur listing — including 20+ year old vintage fur — is banned from August 11, 2026. The rule was published on April 9, 2026, giving sellers a 124-day migration window. Leather, wool, sheepskin, and faux fur remain allowed. Taxidermy requires Etsy pre-approval. The full rule text is in the [Etsy 2026 Animal Fur Policy Update](https://www.etsy.com/legal/policy/animals-and-animal-derived-products).

**Who is affected:** every vintage fur seller, every contemporary fur seller, every seller with a single listing that mentions mink, fox, rabbit, or raccoon. The vintage seller community is the most disrupted — many vintage shops have 30-80% of their inventory in the now-banned category.

**What is still allowed:** leather (cow, pig, sheep, lamb, suede), wool, sheepskin, mohair, animal specimens (taxidermy with pre-approval), faux fur (synthetic, plant-based, recycled fibers), and fiber art without animal hair. The official [Etsy 8/11 Animal Fur Policy: 5 AI Alternatives](/blog/etsy-8-11-animal-fur-policy) guide covers the five-category pivot map and the [Etsy Fur Ban: 35-Day Action Plan](/blog/etsy-fur-ban-35-day-action-plan) covers the migration calendar for sellers with 25+ vintage fur listings. Vintage sellers specifically should also see the [Best AI Tools for Etsy Vintage Sellers](/blog/best-ai-tools-for-etsy-vintage-sellers) guide for the post-8/11 tool stack.

**AI compliance method:** vintage fur sellers are pivoting to faux fur, leather accessories, and upcycled textiles. AI image tools like [Pebblely](https://pebblely.com) and [Midjourney](https://www.midjourney.com/account) help re-shoot the inventory in the new style within a 25-day window, and AI SEO tools like [eRank](https://erank.com/keyword-explorer) identify the post-8/11 search terms buyers are migrating to. The five-alternative pivot map is detailed in the [Etsy 8/11 Animal Fur Policy: 5 AI Alternatives](/blog/etsy-8-11-animal-fur-policy) guide.

## The 5-step AI compliance method

The four policies look separate, but the compliance workflow is the same for all of them. The 5-step method below is what we recommend to every Etsy seller navigating 2026. Each step has a specific AI tool attached to it — none of the steps require a paid tool, but the paid tiers save time and improve accuracy.

### Step 1 — Policy identification (30 minutes)

Open the official [Etsy Seller Handbook](https://www.etsy.com/seller-handbook/artificial-intelligence-and-ip) and tag every listing in your shop against the four 2026 policies. The fastest way to do this is to copy your full inventory into a spreadsheet and add four columns: 6/2 children (Y/N), 6/4 cleanup-affected (Y/N), UK 0.48% affected (Y/N), 8/11 fur affected (Y/N). The output is a policy-impact matrix that drives steps 2 through 5.

If you have 100+ listings, AI can do the tagging in under 10 minutes. Export your inventory as a CSV, run it through ChatGPT or Claude with the prompt "tag each listing against these four Etsy 2026 policies," and verify the output. The tagging accuracy from GPT-4 or Claude 3.5 is around 95% for obvious cases and drops to 80% for ambiguous listings (e.g., "nursery wall art" — does "nursery" trigger children-related? Yes.).

### Step 2 — Impact scoring (45 minutes)

For every listing tagged Y in step 1, score the impact on a 1-5 scale. 5 = listing is in imminent danger of removal (e.g., vintage mink coat in the 8/11 fur ban window). 1 = listing is barely affected (e.g., a single keyword overlap with the children policy that needs a one-word edit). Sort the matrix by impact score descending — that becomes your migration priority list.

A vintage shop with 200 listings might end up with 60 listings at impact 5, 40 at impact 4, 30 at impact 3, 20 at impact 2, and 50 at impact 1. The 60 impact-5 listings get migrated in week 1, the 40 impact-4 in week 2, and so on. The reason to score is that not every listing needs equal attention, and the highest-impact listings are the ones that get you suspended first.

### Step 3 — Action checklist (2 hours per impact-5 listing)

For each impact-5 listing, write a 5-row action checklist: (1) what needs to change in the title, (2) what needs to change in the description, (3) what needs to change in the tags, (4) what needs to change in the imagery, (5) what needs to change in the category or attributes. Run the revised listing through [Roketfy's Listing Checker](https://roketfy.com/listing-checker) to confirm the policy flag is gone before publishing.

For impact-3 and below, the checklist is simpler — often a single title edit or a single tag swap. The principle is to never re-publish a listing without re-running the compliance scan, because Etsy's enforcement is automated and does not give warnings on the first violation after the policy effective date.

### Step 4 — Tool stack (1 hour to set up, then ongoing)

The 4-category tool stack for 2026 compliance is: AI listing tools (Roketfy, Alura, EtsyHunt), AI SEO tools (eRank, Marmalead, Sale Samurai), AI product photo tools (Pebblely, Midjourney, Mockey), and AI detection / compliance scanning tools (Originality.ai, Roketfy Listing Checker, EtsyHunt monitor). The full breakdown of all 12 tools is in the next section.

The order to set them up is: eRank first (free, 5-minute setup, gives you the keyword baseline), then ChatGPT or Claude (free, gives you the batch drafting capability), then [Roketfy](https://roketfy.com/listing-checker) (free tier, gives you the compliance scan), then [Originality.ai](https://originality.ai/) (7-day free trial, gives you the AI detection check), then [EtsyHunt](https://etsyhunt.com/) (free, gives you the monitoring loop). Total setup time is around 60 minutes, total monthly cost is $30-50 for paid tiers.

### Step 5 — Monitoring loop (30 minutes per week)

Etsy enforces the 2026 policies on a rolling basis, not a single cutoff date. The 8/11 fur ban enforcement, for example, started with automated scans on August 12, 2026 but the first violation warnings did not go out until September 1, 2026. To stay ahead, run a four-step monitoring loop every week: (1) re-run [Roketfy Listing Checker](https://roketfy.com/listing-checker) on your top 20 listings, (2) check [eRank](https://erank.com/keyword-explorer) for new policy-related search trends, (3) check [EtsyHunt](https://etsyhunt.com/) for competitor listing changes, (4) check your Etsy shop dashboard for any policy notifications.

The 30-minute weekly loop is the single highest-ROI compliance activity in 2026. Sellers who skip the loop discover policy violations only when Etsy removes the listing — by which point the search ranking and conversion data are already lost.
