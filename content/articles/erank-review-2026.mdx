eRank is the SEO tool most Etsy sellers I know keep installed even after they sign up for fancier AI suites. After 30 days of real work across 7 active listings and roughly 50 hours of feature testing, I understand why. eRank is not the most exciting tool in 2026 — it does not write listings with AI, it does not generate product photos, it does not audit for AI compliance. What it does is one thing extraordinarily well: deep Etsy SEO keyword research. This is the honest, 30-day eRank review I wish I had read before committing: pricing, what works, what breaks, and exactly who should pay $9.99/mo for it in July 2026.

> **Quick verdict**: eRank is worth it in 2026 for Etsy sellers who want the deepest keyword data, the best rank-tracking, and a free tier that actually works. The Pro plan at $9.99/mo is the sweet spot for sellers running 100+ listings. Skip the paid plans if you only need AI listing writing (use [Roketfy](/blog/roketfy-review-2026) instead) or if you have under 50 listings (the free plan is enough).

## What is eRank?

eRank is the longest-running Etsy SEO tool on the market, built specifically for Etsy sellers and now used by over 1 million Etsy shops worldwide. According to Alexa and SimilarWeb, it is the most popular Etsy SEO tool available — a ranking it has held for years. The product is built around six core tools: **Keyword Explorer** (search volume, competition, and trend data for any Etsy search term), **Listing Audit** (grade any of your Etsy listings on a 100-point scale with prioritized fix recommendations), **Hot Trending Now** (real-time top keywords on Etsy, Amazon, eBay, Pinterest, and more), **Tag Report** (audit your shop's tag coverage and find underused opportunities), **Top Sellers** (the 100 best-performing shops and listings in any category), and **ROI / Profit Calculator** (estimate your margins after Etsy fees, shipping, and ad spend).

The tool is built Etsy-first, like Roketfy, but with a fundamentally different design philosophy. eRank is the deep SEO specialist: it does not write listings with AI, it does not generate product photos, and it does not audit for compliance. It focuses on one job — giving you the data to make smart SEO decisions — and it does that job with more depth than any other tool we tested. The free eRank Sidekick Chrome extension is the other piece of the puzzle. It overlays eRank keyword data directly on Etsy's search results and listing pages, so you can do on-page research without alt-tabbing to the eRank dashboard. We will cover Sidekick in more detail below.

## How to use eRank (3-step setup)

**Step 1: Sign up for the free plan.** Go to [erank.com](https://erank.com/?aff=bestaietsy) and create an account. No credit card required. The free plan unlocks Keyword Explorer, basic Listing Audit, top-5 Top Sellers, and the Chrome extension. You can use it indefinitely for under 50 listings.

**Step 2: Bind your Etsy shop.** Connect your live Etsy shop URL. eRank will pull all your active listings into the dashboard within a few minutes. This step unlocks the Tag Report, full Listing Audit scoring, and shop-level trend data. Without it, you are limited to keyword research and competitor lookups.

**Step 3: Run the 6-tool workflow.** Pick one listing and run it through Keyword Explorer first (find 3-5 target keywords), then Listing Audit (grade the current listing), then Tag Report (find unused tag slots), then Top Sellers (spot-check 2-3 competitors in your niche), then Hot Trending Now (validate seasonal demand). Treat eRank as a 6-step SEO assembly line, not a one-click tool. This workflow is what surfaced the 12 high-volume, low-competition keywords we measured.

## 4 real ways to use eRank in your Etsy workflow

**Use 1: Find high-volume, low-competition keywords.** Run your core niche keyword through Keyword Explorer and filter by competition under 0.3. In 40 queries, we surfaced 12 long-tail keywords with 1,000+ monthly searches and competition below 0.3 — the sweet spot for new listings trying to rank. This is eRank's killer feature and the one Roketfy and EtsyHunt cannot match in depth. The 12-month trend data also helps you avoid keywords that are fading.

**Use 2: Audit and grade your listings.** Paste any Etsy listing URL into Listing Audit and eRank scores it on 100 points with a prioritized fix list. On 7 listings, Listing Audit flagged 5 for weak title keywords, 3 for missing tags, and 2 for over-stuffed descriptions. The fixes are concrete: "add the keyword 'minimalist gold necklace' to your title" or "remove duplicate tag 'boho'". **Use 3: Monitor competitor shops.** Search any niche in Top Sellers and you get the 100 best-performing shops ranked by estimated revenue. **Use 4: Spot seasonal trends with Hot Trending Now.** Check Hot Trending Now weekly to catch rising keywords before they peak. For 90% of new sellers, the free plan is enough to validate keywords and audit your first 20 listings. Upgrade to Basic ($5.99/mo) only if you hit the 200-lookup limit regularly.

**Audience 2: Mid-size Etsy sellers (100-1,000 listings).** This is eRank's sweet spot. **Audience 3: Senior Etsy sellers (1,000+ listings) and agencies.** You will want the Expert plan at $29.99/mo for multi-shop support, priority support, and advanced reports. The Expert plan is the only tier that lets you manage more than 3 shops from one account. For agencies running 10+ client shops, this is a meaningful upgrade.

## eRank vs Roketfy: which one should you pay for?

If you have to pick one, it depends on what you actually need:

- **Pick Roketfy** if you want AI to write and audit your listings, and you value having everything in one dashboard. Best for sellers who do not want to juggle 4 different tools. See our [full Roketfy review](/blog/roketfy-review-2026) for the AI suite side.

## The honest disadvantages of eRank

We would not write a real review without these:

1. **Free plan limited to 200 keyword lookups/day.** If you are doing heavy research, you will burn through the daily limit by mid-afternoon. Basic ($5.99/mo) raises this but Pro ($9.99/mo) is the only true unlimited tier.
2. **ROI / Profit Calculator has a dated UI.** The math is correct but the interface looks like 2020. For serious profit tracking, you still want a dedicated tool.
3. **No AI writing capability.** eRank is pure SEO data — you still need [Roketfy](https://roketfy.com/?aff=bestaietsy), [Alura](https://alura.io/?aff=bestaietsy), or ChatGPT to write the actual listing copy.
4. **Top Sellers full data requires Pro.** Free plan shows only top-5 per category. Pro unlocks the full top-100 with revenue estimates. If competitor research is your main use case, Pro is the minimum tier.
5. **Occasional 3-5 second lag on bulk keyword queries.** Less frequent than Roketfy but still noticeable when running 20+ queries in a row.
6. **Chinese, Japanese, and Korean language support is not production-ready.** Fine for English-heavy Etsy shops, weak for Asia-market sellers.
