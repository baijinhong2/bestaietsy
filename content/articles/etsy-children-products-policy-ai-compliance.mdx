## What changed on June 2, 2026

Etsy announced the Children & Infant Products Policy on May 27, 2026, with an effective date six days later. The framework covers all products intended for children ages 0 to 14 and applies on top of Etsy's existing handmade, vintage, and prohibited-items policies.

**Five compliance requirements now mandatory on every children's listing:**

1. **Material composition disclosure** — full breakdown of all materials (e.g., "100% organic cotton shell, recycled polyester fill, OEKO-TEX certified dyes"). Generic terms like "fabric" or "soft material" are flagged.
2. **Applicable safety standard reference** — sellers must cite the relevant standard: ASTM F963 (US toys), EN 71 (EU toys), CPSIA (US consumer products), or AS/NZS ISO 8124 (Australia/NZ).
3. **Age recommendation or warning** — either a recommended age range (e.g., "suitable for ages 3+") or an exclusionary warning (e.g., "not suitable for children under 36 months").
4. **Hazard warnings** — explicit warnings for choking (small parts), strangulation (cords/straps), chemical (phthalates, lead), and sharp edges. Warnings must be visible in the description, not buried in attributes.
5. **Manufacturing origin for infant products** — infant items (under 36 months) must include traceable manufacturing origin, even for handmade items.

**Four hazard categories are now banned outright:**

- Choking hazards: small parts detachable under reasonable force, balls under 1.75 inches diameter for under-3-year-olds
- Chemical risks: lead content above 100 ppm, phthalates above 0.1% by weight
- Physical hazards: sharp edges or points accessible to children, rigid points
- Strangulation hazards: cords, straps, or loops longer than 7 inches on products for under-12-month infants

The policy explicitly states that **handmade status does not exempt** a children's product. A handmade wooden teether still needs the same disclosures as a mass-produced competitor.

## Who's affected

**Directly affected** (the largest cohort): sellers in kids' clothing, kids' toys, nursery decor, baby accessories, plush toys, teething products, children's jewelry, kids' bedding, bath toys, walkwear, and stroller accessories. Etsy has not published seller counts by category, but kids' products represent an estimated 15-20% of total Etsy GMV.

**Indirectly affected**: craft supply sellers whose supplies are marketed for children's use (e.g., "kids' craft kits"), digital download sellers producing printable children's items (e.g., nursery art, party printables), and custom-orders sellers accepting children's projects (e.g., personalized baby blankets).

**Not affected**: adult-only products even with child-themed marketing (e.g., "mama bear" t-shirts), pet products, and household items not intended for children. These categories retain standard Etsy compliance rules without the children-specific disclosures.

The hardest-hit cohort is **solo parent-side-hustle sellers** — typically Etsy sellers who launched in the 2020-2023 boom to monetize a side craft. They often have 50-300 children's product listings, no full-time compliance staff, and limited budget for policy audits. The AI compliance workflow below is built for this profile.

## What's allowed vs. banned

The full compliance matrix breaks into three buckets.

**Allowed without restriction** (standard handmade, vintage, or craft supply categories, provided the children-specific disclosures are present): clothing, toys, accessories, jewelry, bedding, plush items, strollers, walkers, and bath items — when the five mandatory disclosures and the four hazard restrictions are met.

**Banned outright regardless of disclosure**: items containing banned chemicals (lead above threshold, phthalates above 0.1%), small parts that detach under 1.34 lbf pull force for under-3-year-olds, products with cord/strap length over 7 inches marketed to under-12-month infants, products with accessible sharp edges after a 1.5 lbf pull test, and children's products without traceable manufacturing origin for infant items.

**Gray area requiring judgment**: upcycled children's items (allowed if the source material is disclosed and post-upcycling it meets hazard rules), multi-use products (covered if marketed for children), and "heirloom" or "collectible" children's items (covered if functional, exempt if clearly decorative). When in doubt, Etsy recommends conservative compliance: add all five disclosures plus the most restrictive hazard warning.
